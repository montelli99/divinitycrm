 DEED IN LIEU OF FORECLOSURE ADDENDUM TO PURCHASE AGREEMENT

This Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure Addendum (the "Addendum") is incorporated into and made a part of the Purchase Agreement dated [EFFECTIVE_DATE] by and between [SELLER_NAME] ("Seller") and [BUYER_NAME] ("Buyer") for the property located at [PROPERTY_ADDRESS].

1. DEED IN LIEU AUTHORIZATION. At close of escrow, Buyer shall execute and deliver to Seller (or Seller's escrow agent) a deed in lieu of foreclosure (the "DIL Deed"), held in escrow. The DIL Deed conveys the Property back to Seller without the need for foreclosure proceedings.

2. TRIGGER CONDITIONS. The DIL Deed shall be released from escrow and recorded by Seller if any of the following occur:
   (a) Buyer fails to make any payment due to Seller or to the existing loan servicer within 30 days of the due date;
   (b) Buyer abandons the Property;
   (c) Buyer files for bankruptcy;
   (d) Buyer transfers or attempts to transfer the Property in violation of the Purchase Agreement.

3. CURE PERIOD. Before the DIL Deed is recorded:
   (a) Seller shall deliver written notice of default to Buyer at Buyer's last known address and email;
   (b) Buyer has 15 days from receipt of notice to cure the default by bringing all payments current, including any late fees;
   (c) If Buyer cures within 15 days, the DIL Deed remains in escrow and the Purchase Agreement continues in effect;
   (d) If Buyer fails to cure within 15 days, Seller may record the DIL Deed and regain ownership of the Property.

4. EFFECT OF DIL DEED. Upon recording of the DIL Deed:
   (a) All of Buyer's interest in the Property terminates;
   (b) Seller regains ownership of the Property, subject only to the existing loan(s) and liens recorded prior to Buyer's deed of trust;
   (c) All improvements, renovations, and repairs made by Buyer remain with the Property;
   (d) Seller is not responsible for reimbursing Buyer for any investments, improvements, or payments made prior to the default.

5. COOPERATION. Buyer agrees to execute any additional documents necessary to effectuate the deed in lieu of foreclosure, including but not limited to:
   (a) A rescission deed;
   (b) An affidavit of default;
   (c) A release of all claims against the Property and Seller.

6. SURVIVAL. This Addendum survives the close of escrow and remains in effect until the seller financing is paid in full or the DIL Deed is recorded.
